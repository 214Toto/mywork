Skin Pthology Roadmap

1. ACE Inhibitors

Examples: Chronic irritation, neoplastic growths

Mechanism: Hyperplasia of stratum malpighii (basal + spinosum layers) → epidermal thickening

Key feature: Regular (uniform) or irregular (uneven) thickening of epidermis.

Examples: Pemphigus vulgaris, blistering disorders

Mechanism: Loss of desmosomal connections between keratinocytes → intraepidermal blister formation

Key feature: Skin cells detach, forming fluid-filled spaces.

Examples: Actinic keratosis, squamous cell carcinoma

Mechanism: Abnormal keratinocyte maturation → rounded cells with pyknotic nuclei

Key feature: Premalignant/malignant marker; rarely seen in benign conditions.

Examples: Eczema, allergic contact dermatitis

Mechanism: Intercellular edema separates keratinocytes → sponge-like appearance

Key feature: Hallmark of inflammatory skin conditions.

Examples: Calluses, warts, ichthyosis

Mechanism: Excessive keratin production → thickened stratum corneum

Key feature: May involve abnormal keratin quality.

Examples: Psoriasis, actinic keratosis

Mechanism: Retained nuclei in stratum corneum due to rapid keratinocyte turnover

Key feature: Normal on mucosal surfaces; abnormal on skin.

Examples: Seborrheic keratosis, viral warts

Mechanism: Hyperplasia of papillary dermis → elongated dermal papillae

Key feature: Creates wavy skin architecture.

Examples: HPV-induced warts

Mechanism: Vacuolated koilocytes + parakeratotic stratum corneum

Key feature: Thickened horny layer with nucleated cells.

Examples: Psoriasis, chronic dermatitis

Mechanism: Uniform epidermal hyperplasia → regular acanthosis

Key feature: Resembles psoriasis histologically.

Examples: Lichen planus, post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation

Mechanism: Basal layer damage → melanin phagocytosis by dermal melanophages

Key feature: Dermal pigment deposition after epidermal injury.

Examples: Eczema, psoriasis, pemphigus

Mechanism: Varies by type (genetic, inflammatory, infectious, etc.)

Key feature: Umbrella term for all skin disorders.

Side Effects: Dry cough, hyperkalemia, angioedema

2. Beta Blockers

Examples: Carvedilol, Bisoprolol, Metoprolol succinate

Mechanism: Block β1-adrenergic receptors → reduce sympathetic overactivity

Important: Start at low dose and titrate slowly in stable patients

Key Points:

  • Only use evidence-based beta blockers in HF
  • May initially worsen symptoms before improvement
  • Contraindicated in acute decompensated HF

3. Definition of Terms

Examples: Spironolactone, Eplerenone

Mechanism: Block aldosterone effects → reduce sodium retention and fibrosis

Monitoring Required: Potassium levels (risk of hyperkalemia), renal function

Clinical Use:

  • NYHA Class II-IV with reduced EF
  • Post-MI with reduced EF and symptoms

4. ARNI (Sacubitril/Valsartan)

Brand Name: Entresto

Dual Mechanism:

  • Sacubitril: Inhibits neprilysin → ↑ natriuretic peptides
  • Valsartan: ARB effects
PARADIGM-HF Trial: Showed superiority over enalapril in reducing CV death and HF hospitalization

Switching Protocol:

  1. Stop ACE inhibitor
  2. 36-hour washout period
  3. Start ARNI at low dose

5. Diuretics

Loop Diuretics: Furosemide, Bumetanide, Torsemide

Thiazides: Hydrochlorothiazide, Chlorthalidone (for mild HF)

Clinical Use:

  • Symptom relief (no mortality benefit)
  • Adjust dose to maintain euvolemia
  • Combine with salt restriction
Adverse Effects: Electrolyte disturbances, dehydration, ototoxicity (loops), gout, photosensitivity (thiazides)

6. SGLT2 Inhibitors

Examples: Dapagliflozin, Empagliflozin

Mechanism: Inhibit renal glucose reabsorption → osmotic diuresis, may improve myocardial metabolism

Key Trials:

  • DAPA-HF: Dapagliflozin in HFrEF
  • EMPEROR-Reduced: Empagliflozin in HFrEF
  • EMPEROR-Preserved: Empagliflozin in HFpEF
Advantage: Benefits seen regardless of diabetes status

7. Additional Therapies

Digoxin: Symptom relief in sinus rhythm, no mortality benefit

Ivabradine: For patients with HR >70 on max BB dose

Hydralazine/ISDN: Especially beneficial in African Americans

Device Therapy:

  • ICD for primary/secondary prevention
  • CRT for specific QRS patterns
